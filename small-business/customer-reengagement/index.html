The Problem

Your Best Leads Already Know You. You Just Stopped Talking to Them.

The most expensive customer acquisition you'll ever do is the first time someone hires you. After that, you have something far more valuable than a prospect: a customer who already trusts your work, knows your pricing, and has experienced your service.

Most service businesses treat that relationship as a closed transaction rather than the ongoing asset it is. The job gets done, the invoice gets paid, and the customer goes into a spreadsheet, or a scheduling app, or a sticky note, and doesn't hear from you again until they call with a problem.

In the Keystone Plumbing dataset of 850 customers and 2,400 jobs, 44% of customers had not been contacted in over 14 months. Many of those were ideal re-engagement candidates, seasonal service needs, long-overdue maintenance, equipment at end-of-life. Revenue the business had already earned the right to, sitting uncollected.

The Re-Engagement Engine analyzes your job history, identifies customers who are overdue, and sends personalized outreach that reads like it came from you, not a mass marketing blast.

How It Works

Analyze. Personalize. Convert.

01

Analyze

We run your customer and job history to identify who's overdue, HVAC tune-ups, annual drain cleans, spring lawn treatments. Highest-value customers surface first. You approve the list before anything goes out.

02

Personalize

Outreach goes out by text and email, timed to the season and the service. Each message references the customer's actual history with you. No generic blasts, no "Dear Valued Customer" copy.

03

Convert

Customers respond, jobs get scheduled. You see exactly which campaigns drove which bookings, what revenue they generated, and who's coming back vs. who needs a different approach next time.
