The Problem

Every Unanswered Call Is a Job You Gave Away

In a representative dataset of 3,200 inbound calls across a mid-size plumbing operation, 34% went unanswered — mostly after 5 PM, on weekends, and during peak field hours when every tech was already on a job.

At an average ticket of $415, that gap represents over $450,000 in annual revenue that walked straight to a competitor. Not because the business was bad at its work. Because nobody picked up the phone.

The traditional fixes don't work. Answering services are expensive, inconsistent, and can't actually book jobs. Hiring a dispatcher adds $40,000–$60,000 in payroll before they answer a single after-hours call. And voicemail? Callers with a leaking pipe at 9 PM don't leave voicemails — they call the next number on the list.

The problem isn't that your team doesn't care. It's that humans can't be everywhere at once. AI can.

How It Works

Three Steps. Zero Missed Calls.

01

Intercept

Every call your team can't answer — after hours, during jobs, at lunch — is picked up by the AI receptionist within two rings. It introduces itself as your business and gets right to it.

02

Qualify

It gathers the caller's name, service type, location, scheduling preference, and urgency — the same information your best dispatcher would collect, every time, without fail.

03

Hand Off

Emergency calls get a live text to you immediately. All others are logged, summarized, and ready to schedule first thing the next morning. Nothing falls through the cracks.
