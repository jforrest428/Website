The Problem

Your Reviews Are Running Your Business. You're Just Not Reading Them.

88% of consumers read reviews before contacting a local service business. The businesses showing up at the top of Google Maps have one thing in common: they respond to reviews consistently, professionally, and quickly.

Most service owners know they should respond. They intend to. But between dispatching techs, handling callbacks, and managing the day-to-day, review responses fall to the bottom of the list. The result: a 3-star review that goes unanswered for three weeks, a string of glowing 5-star reviews with no acknowledgment, and a profile that quietly signals to potential customers that nobody's paying attention.

In the Keystone Plumbing dataset, fewer than 20% of reviews received a response within 30 days. For the ones that were negative, the response rate was even lower. That's the profile a competitor with a consistent reply cadence easily beats, even if the underlying service quality is identical.

Review management isn't glamorous work, but it's one of the highest-leverage things a local business can do for its Google ranking and its conversion rate. The Reputation Manager does it automatically, in your voice, on your behalf.
