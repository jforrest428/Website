The Problem

You Sent the Estimate. Then Nothing.

Industry data shows that 44% of salespeople give up after one follow-up. Meanwhile, 80% of sales require five or more touchpoints. For service businesses sending estimates, this gap costs real money on every quote that goes cold.

Here's how it usually goes: you spend time on a job site, put together a fair estimate, send it over, and wait. The customer got busy. Maybe they're comparing quotes. Maybe they want to pull the trigger but just haven't gotten around to it. A single follow-up text from you — sent 48 hours after the estimate — would often close the job. But you're running a crew, dispatching techs, and handling the day-to-day. Following up on a cold estimate is the last thing on your list.

The result: good leads die in your inbox. Jobs you could have won go to the competitor who happened to call at the right moment.

The Estimate Follow-Up Automation puts a disciplined, AI-written follow-up sequence on every estimate you send. Day 2, Day 5, Day 10 — each message is written fresh, references the specific estimate, and sounds like you wrote it personally. Not a form letter. Not a mass text. A human follow-up, at scale.

How It Works

Send the Estimate. The System Does the Rest.

01

Estimate Sent

You send an estimate as you normally would — through your CRM, email, or a simple export. The follow-up engine logs it and starts the clock.

02

Sequence Begins

Three personalized follow-ups are written automatically: a warm check-in at Day 2, a value reminder at Day 5, and a low-pressure close at Day 10. Each references the specific job, the estimate amount, and your availability. Delivered via SMS, email, or both.

03

Reply = Booked

When the customer responds, the sequence stops and you get a notification. They've already warmed up through three personal touchpoints — they're ready to book. Estimates marked as won or lost are automatically removed from the sequence.

Zero Owner Time

You write the estimate once. Every follow-up happens automatically. No manual reminders, no checking a spreadsheet, no awkward "just following up" calls. Your time stays on the jobs you've already won.

Personal

Generic follow-ups get ignored. Each message references the specific scope of work, the customer's name, and a realistic timeframe — "just wanted to check in on the water heater quote from Tuesday." That specificity drives replies.
