The Problem

They Didn't Leave a Voicemail. They Called the Next Plumber.

Studies consistently show that 85% of callers who reach voicemail do not leave a message. For a service business missing 5 calls a week at an average ticket of $600, that's $156,000 a year quietly walking out the door.

The problem isn't just the missed call itself — it's the timing. When someone's toilet is backing up or their water heater is dead, they need help now. The average caller gives you one shot. If your phone rings out, they're already scrolling to the next result. There's no second chance, no voicemail, no callback.

In the Keystone Plumbing dataset — 3,200 inbound calls over two years — 29% went unanswered. Nearly all of those happened after 5 PM, on weekends, and during peak mid-day field hours when every technician was already on a job. Those are exactly the moments when a customer's need is most urgent and their patience is shortest.

The Missed-Call SMS Responder closes that gap. Before the caller has time to open a new tab, they have a text from you — personal, warm, and offering to schedule. Not a robot. Not a voicemail prompt. A real message that sounds like your office sent it.

How It Works

Miss the Call. Win the Job.

01

Call Goes Unanswered

Your line rings out — you're on a job, after hours, or all techs are dispatched. Twilio detects the no-answer within seconds and triggers the responder automatically.

02

AI Writes the Text

Within 30 seconds, a personalized SMS is written in your voice. If the caller is in your CRM, their name and last service history are included. After-hours calls get a different tone than midday calls. No templates — every message is written fresh.

03

They Reply. You Book.

The caller gets a friendly, human text before they have time to call someone else. They reply, you respond, and the job is booked — all from a conversation that started with a missed call.
