Case Study

Built on Real Data. Here's What It Showed.

Keystone Plumbing is a representative sample business used to design and demonstrate every AI tool in this suite. The dataset is realistic, the numbers are real, and the findings apply to most service businesses at similar scale.

About this case study: Keystone Plumbing is a representative sample business, not a specific client. It was constructed using realistic operational parameters for a mid-size residential plumbing company: 850 active customers, 2,400 completed jobs, and 3,200 inbound calls tracked over a 12-month period. The dataset was built to reflect patterns common across the trades, and was used to design, configure, and validate each AI product in this suite. Results shown reflect what the data revealed, not projected or hypothetical outcomes.

What the Data Revealed

Four Pain Points. Four Leaks in the Revenue Bucket.

The audit ran across call logs, job history, customer records, and review activity. Here's what surfaced, and which tool was built to address it.

01

34% of Inbound Calls Went Unanswered

Out of 3,200 inbound calls over 12 months, 1,088 went to voicemail or were abandoned. The pattern was consistent: afternoons during peak field hours, evenings after 5 PM, and all day Saturday. The business was fully operational, the phones just weren't being answered.

At $415 per job and an industry average booking rate for captured calls of roughly 60%, that gap represents approximately $270,000 in recoverable annual revenue from calls the business never had a chance to win.

Solution

24/7 AI Voice Receptionist

Intercepts every unanswered call within two rings. Qualifies the lead, captures contact info and service type, escalates emergencies, and hands a clean summary to the team each morning. Nothing hits voicemail.

02

44% of Customers Hadn't Been Contacted in 14+ Months

Of 850 active customers, 374 had not received any outreach, promotional, maintenance reminder, or follow-up, in over 14 months. Analysis of their job history showed many were statistically overdue for service: drain maintenance, water heater inspections, seasonal prep.

This dormant segment represented an estimated $155,000 in serviceable revenue that could be reactivated through a single targeted outreach campaign, at zero acquisition cost, because the relationship already existed.

Solution

Customer Re-Engagement Engine

Analyzes job history to identify who's overdue, builds personalized outreach sequences timed to season and service type, and converts dormant customers into booked jobs, without a single ad dollar spent.

See This Product →
03

Fewer Than 1 in 5 Reviews Received a Response

Review activity over the 12-month period included 142 new reviews across Google and Yelp. Of those, only 26 received a response from the business, and of the 18 reviews rated 3 stars or below, none received a response within 14 days. Three were never addressed at all.

Keystone's average star rating across platforms was 4.1. Three direct competitors in the same service area, with comparable ratings, were consistently outranking it in local search results. The difference: response rate and recency. Both are Google ranking signals.

Solution

Review Reply & Reputation Manager

Monitors all connected review platforms 24/7, drafts professional responses within minutes, routes them for one-tap approval, and flags negative reviews with a suggested recovery action before they cost you the next job.

See This Product →
04

The Owner Had No Daily Operational Picture

Call data lived in one system. Job history in another. Review activity required logging into three different platforms. Revenue trends required pulling from the accounting software. No single view existed, which meant operational decisions were made on instinct and lag, often weeks after a problem had developed.

In one stretch of the dataset, call booking rates dropped 18% over three weeks before the dip was noticed. By the time the pattern was visible, two techs had already had light weeks that could have been filled if the signal had come earlier.

Solution

Owner's Daily Briefing

Pulls data from every connected source each morning and delivers a plain-English summary by 7 AM, call volume, booking rate, review activity, outstanding follow-ups, anything that looks unusual. Know before the day starts.
