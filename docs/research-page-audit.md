# Research Page Audit — forrestintelligence.com/research/
**Conducted:** April 10, 2026  
**Auditor:** Claude Code (via Playwright MCP + source review)  
**Scope:** /research/ full page + all linked destinations

---

## 1. Executive Summary — Top 5 Highest-Impact Changes

1. **Add a pricing section** (CRITICAL) — The page has zero pricing information. The FAQ deflects with "based on scope." Researchers, especially grad students, assume "too expensive" and bounce. A visible 3-tier pricing block with starting numbers would immediately reduce friction and qualify leads better.

2. **Rewrite the hero to target researchers specifically** — "Statistical Support for Researchers & Organizations" buries the lead and dilutes the audience. A PhD student doesn't see themselves in "Organizations." The headline needs to call out PhD students, postdocs, and faculty directly, and the sub-copy needs to speak to their actual pain ("your methodology chapter," "your committee") not generic consulting language.

3. **Add a testimonials section** — There is zero social proof from actual researchers. No quotes, no case stats, no anonymized outcomes. For an academic audience that heavily weights peer opinion and institutional credibility, this is a conversion killer. Even 2–3 anonymized testimonials ("PhD Candidate, Nursing Science, Penn State") would meaningfully increase trust.

4. **Enhance credentials and the About section** — The Wharton Executive Education work (Aramco, T-Mobile, Apple programs) is the single strongest credibility signal on the page, but it's described generically as "a part-time research role." The M.S. in Business Intelligence & Analytics (Data Science specialization) from SJU is not mentioned in the about section at all. The SJU affiliation still reads "incoming Adjunct Instructor" — that course is currently underway.

5. **Add a specific methods/tools list** — Researchers want to know you can handle *their* type of analysis before they contact you. The current page mentions R, Python, Excel, SPSS, and ANOVA — that's it. Researchers searching for "SEM consultant" or "HLM dissertation help" won't find relevance here. A compact methods list (SEM, HLM, factor analysis, mediation/moderation, power analysis, etc.) signals real methodological depth.

## 2. What's Working Well

- **Affiliations section** — Penn/Wharton, Duquesne, SJU cards are well-designed and immediately credible. The badge + institution + description format is clean and trustworthy.
- **"Sound Familiar?" problem cards** — The direct-quote format ("I don't know which test to use") creates strong resonance with dissertation students. This section earns empathy quickly.
- **4-step process section** — "Free call → Written scope → Analysis → Follow-up" is transparent, low-risk, and directly addresses researcher hesitation about getting locked into an engagement.
- **Academic integrity FAQ items** — Answering "do you write papers for students?" and "is this IRB-allowed?" upfront is exactly right. These are the first two questions in every grad student's head.
- **Contact form** — Clean, functional. Email visible. "All inquiries are confidential" note is important for researchers worried about advisor perception.
- **Mobile layout** — Renders cleanly at 375px. Hero, affiliations, and cards all stack correctly with no overflow.
- **Design** — The navy/gold palette reads as professional and academic without feeling like a law firm. Appropriate for the audience.
- **Flat-rate pricing framing** — The mention of "flat-rate proposal" in Process Step 2 is good. Researchers hate hourly billing anxiety. But it needs to be paired with actual numbers somewhere.

## 6. Pricing Recommendations

### New Section: Engagement Options

**Tier 1 — Methodology Session**
- **Starting at $150 / session**
- 1–2 hour focused consultation
- For: Any researcher who needs targeted help with analysis design, method selection, interpretation, or troubleshooting
- Best for: PhD students who need a few hours of guidance before running their own analysis
- No long commitment. Book as needed.

**Tier 2 — Project Support** *(Most Requested)*
- **Starting at $450 / project** (scoped and quoted before work begins)
- Flat-fee, scope-defined engagement
- For: Dissertation chapters, thesis analysis, manuscript stats, grant data reports
- Includes: Full analysis, documented output, interpretation write-up, and one follow-up session
- Best for: Researchers who need complete analysis support for a defined deliverable

**Tier 3 — Research Retainer**
- **Starting at $800 / month**
- Ongoing support across multiple projects or phases
- For: Labs, faculty PIs, or multi-phase research projects
- Includes: Priority scheduling, regular check-ins, ad-hoc analysis support
- Best for: Faculty or labs that need continuous analytical capacity without hiring full-time

**Key notes for implementation:**
- Add "Academic discount available — ask during your consult" to make students feel seen without being condescending
- Add "Project-based pricing can be documented for grant reimbursement" to signal faculty-friendliness
- The free 15-minute call is the entry point to all tiers — keep it prominent

## 11. Conversion Path Map

### Current Path
1. Land on hero → 2 CTAs (Consultation / View Services)
2. Scroll → Affiliations (credibility, no CTA)
3. Scroll → Problem cards (empathy, no CTA)
4. Scroll → Services → "Request a Consultation" CTA (navy button)
5. Scroll → Process → "Book Your Free 15-Minute Call" CTA (gold button)
6. Scroll → About → "Work With Me" CTA
7. Scroll → FAQ (no CTA)
8. Land on → Contact form → submit

**Issues:**
- 3 different CTA labels for the same action (Request a Consultation / Book a 15-Min Call / Work With Me) — creates confusion about what happens when you click
- FAQ has no CTA — missed warm-intent moment (someone reading FAQs is highly interested)
- No sticky CTA or persistent conversion path for mobile

### Recommended Path
- Standardize primary CTA to: "Get a Free Consultation" or "Book a Free 15-Min Call" — pick one label, use it everywhere
- Add a CTA after the FAQ section
- Consider adding a sticky "Book a Free Call" button on mobile scroll
